Range Order

What is a Range Order?​

A Range Order in Anarkoswap leverages Uniswap v3's concentrated liquidity feature to allow users to provide liquidity within a specific price range. This lets liquidity providers act like limit orders by concentrating their capital only where they want their liquidity to be active.

How Range Orders Work​

  • Instead of providing liquidity across the entire price curve, users specify a price range for their liquidity position.
  • Within this range, the liquidity is active and earns swap fees.
  • Outside the range, the liquidity is inactive and does not participate in swaps.
  • This mechanism allows liquidity providers to target specific price bands, increasing capital efficiency and potential returns.

Benefits​

  • Capital Efficiency: By concentrating liquidity, providers earn more fees with less capital compared to uniform liquidity.
  • Limit Order-like Behavior: Provides price-targeted exposure similar to placing limit orders on centralized exchanges.
  • Flexibility: Users can adjust their ranges to respond to market conditions.

How to Create a Range Order​

  1. Select the token pair and amount to provide liquidity.
  2. Define the minimum and maximum price bounds for your liquidity position.
  3. Confirm the position and submit the transaction.
  4. Your liquidity will be active only within the selected price range.

Limitations​

  • Requires active management to adjust ranges as prices move.
  • Risk of impermanent loss remains within the active range.
  • If the price moves out of the range, liquidity becomes inactive until price returns.

Example Use Cases​

  • Providing liquidity only when a token price is within a target range.
  • Acting as a passive limit order without needing constant monitoring.
  • Enhancing fee earnings by avoiding capital spread thinly across irrelevant price ranges.

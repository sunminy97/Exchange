Concentrated Liquidity

What is Concentrated Liquidity?​

Concentrated Liquidity is a feature introduced in Uniswap v3 that allows liquidity providers (LPs) to allocate their capital within custom price ranges instead of distributing it uniformly across the entire price curve. This results in significantly improved capital efficiency.

How It Works​

  • Traditional AMMs spread liquidity evenly across all price levels, requiring large amounts of capital to provide meaningful liquidity near the current market price.
  • Concentrated Liquidity lets LPs specify a price range where their liquidity will be active.
  • Within the specified range, liquidity is used to facilitate swaps and earn fees.
  • Outside the range, liquidity is inactive and does not participate in trades.

Benefits for Liquidity Providers​

  • Increased Capital Efficiency: LPs can earn more fees with less capital by focusing liquidity where trading occurs.
  • Better Price Execution: Traders benefit from deeper liquidity pools around current prices, resulting in lower slippage.
  • Customizable Risk Exposure: LPs control their price exposure and can tailor strategies based on market expectations.

Implementation in Anarkoswap​

  • Anarkoswap uses Uniswap v3’s concentrated liquidity pools exclusively for liquidity provision.
  • LPs add liquidity by specifying their desired price ranges through the Position Manager contract.
  • The protocol supports adjusting or removing liquidity positions as market conditions change.

Risks and Considerations​

  • Impermanent Loss: Concentrating liquidity increases exposure to price movements within the specified range.
  • Active Management Required: LPs may need to adjust price ranges frequently to maintain effective positions.
  • Inactive Liquidity: If the market price moves outside the LP’s range, liquidity becomes inactive and does not earn fees.

How to Provide Concentrated Liquidity on Anarkoswap​

  1. Choose a token pair and amount to provide liquidity.
  2. Specify the minimum and maximum prices to define your liquidity range.
  3. Confirm and submit your position on-chain.
  4. Monitor and adjust your position as needed based on market price changes.
